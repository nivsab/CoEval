## EXP-002 Ensemble-size ablation

**Hypothesis (original):** reliability increases monotonically with judge count (Condorcet). **Result: falsified, and the falsification is the finding.**

Balanced item set: 10,856 (response, aspect) pairs scored by all 4 judges. Judge addition order: gpt-4o-mini → gpt-3.5-turbo → qwen2p5-1b5 → smollm2-1b7.

| k | panel | ICC(3,k) | ICC(3,1) | Spearman vs full panel [95% CI] |
|---|-------|----------|----------|------------------|
| 1 | gpt-4o-mini | — | — | 0.668 [0.657, 0.679] |
| 2 | +gpt-3.5-turbo | **0.697** | 0.535 | 0.744 [0.734, 0.753] |
| 3 | +qwen2p5-1b5 | 0.452 | 0.216 | 0.928 [0.925, 0.930] |
| 4 | +smollm2-1b7 | 0.405 | 0.145 | 1.000 |

**Root-cause (verified, not a bug):** ICC(3,k) matches the Spearman-Brown identity to 3 decimals (k=2: 2·0.535/(1+0.535)=0.697; k=4: 4·0.145/(1+3·0.145)=0.405). Adding near-random small-model judges (SmolLM2 κ≈0.003) collapses the mean inter-rater correlation faster than averaging compensates, so average-measures reliability *falls*. Conclusion: ensemble *composition*, not size, is first-order — this directly motivates CoEval's consensus-based judge selection. A strong-only panel (EXP-001 with a frontier judge) is expected to recover monotonicity.

## EXP-004 Verbosity bias

Length (tiktoken cl100k_base, recomputed because medium-benchmark-v1 predates the `token_count` field) vs normalised score, one (response, judge) point per response (n=1985), Pearson.

| Judge | r [95% CI] |
|-------|-----------|
| gpt-4o-mini | −0.089 [−0.146, −0.034] |
| gpt-3.5-turbo | −0.177 [−0.225, −0.124] |
| qwen2p5-1b5 | +0.114 [+0.064, +0.163] |
| smollm2-1b7 | +0.234 [+0.187, +0.274] |
| **Ensemble** | **+0.010 [−0.039, +0.057]** |

Strong judges carry no positive verbosity bias (mildly negative); weak judges do. Individual biases are mixed-sign so the signed mean (+0.020) understates the effect; the honest magnitude is **mean\|r\| = 0.153 → ensemble \|r\| = 0.010 (93% reduction)**, with the ensemble CI straddling zero. Supports the bias-cancellation claim.

## EXP-005 Rubric generalization

22 Phase 2 criteria across 4 tasks, all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embeddings, agglomerative clustering (cosine-distance cut 0.45).

- Mean within-task similarity 0.342 > mean cross-task 0.294 (criteria are task-specialised).
- 16 clusters; **1 universal** cluster spanning ≥3 tasks: `completeness`. 11 task-specific singletons.
- Threshold-sensitive: a looser cut would merge `accuracy`-family criteria; report the cut explicitly.
